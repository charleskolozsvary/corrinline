*Note: it is not required that the guidelines described here are followed. `corrinline` will still run, and copy edits will still be made with the benefits of the tool regardless. They may just take longer as a result.*

# Copy edit annotation guidelines
Copy edits can be specified with PDF annotations in many many ways. However, `corrinline` can perform much better (and the copy edits will take less time to compose as a result) if a few simple guidelines are followed. 

## Literal annotations (for autocorrects)
Out of all the PDF annotations, `corrinline` only attempts to perform autocorrections for three of them: strikeouts, replacements, and carets, which means they are interpreted literally. By literal I mean that if `corrinline` does the autocorrection, it will remove, replace, or insert text **exactly as it is marked by the annotation in the PDF**.

As a result, there are few things worth keeping in mind.
1. If you want the autocorrection to be correct, the text selected by the annotation (along with what is written in the comment box) needs to be correct
2. Using "Highlight" as a catch-all prevents many potential autocorrections 
3. Certain typical corrections do not lend themselves to literal changes, and so should not be specified literally

An example of 3. would be tracing text next to a citation into it, or making a theorem heading XTRA. When in doubt, simply highlight what needs to change and write in the comment box what it needs to become. That is much better than, say (for an XTRA copy edit), striking out the period after the heading and inesrting a bold one after the parentheses which follow it.
